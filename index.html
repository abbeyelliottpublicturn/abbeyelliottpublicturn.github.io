Social Media: How It Negatively Affects Your Mental Health

Problematic Social Media Use

Individuals that experience problematic social media use have an extreme desire to be on social media. Individuals that display characteristics of this condition have higher chances of developing addiction and symptoms of depression.

News Article on Problematic Social Media Use

Problematic social media use characteristics intertwine with addiction easily. Watch this video to see how this growing problem is talked about in a news segment aired in 2016.

Addiction to Social Media

Addiction to social media is very possible through many of the features that social media platforms utilize. These include likes, followers, comments, and other interactive features that keep users looking at their notifications.

TED Talk Describing Social Media Addiction

In a TED Talk by Bailey Parnell, she describes how she went on a trip and thought her phone was vibrating when it was not. This shows the impact that even phone vibrations have on our minds.

Increased Symptoms of Depression Caused By Social Media

Through research, it has been shown that symptoms of depression can be increased significantly through negative experiences that users might have on social media use. Fear of missing out and comparison fuel this increase.

How Facebook Can Cause Depression

This video details how the idea of 'Facebook Envy' can cause users to compare themselves to others on the platform. This can further cause the users to experience depression.

Generational Differences in Social Media Use and Mental Health

Through research, it has been shown that many younger generations have higher rates in social media use as well as function very differently than older generations. Are social media and technology to blame for these differences?

Comparing Millennials and Generation Z

The Infographics Show studies some of the main differences between Millennials and Generation Z. This video analyzes how technology and social media have caused generational differences.

About the Author

My name is Abbey Elliott and I am the author of this website. I am 18 years old from Greenville, South Carolina, and creating this website is important to me to better inform others about the potential dangers that social media can have on their mental health. Since social media is so popular in our modern day, we need to be aware of how it can affect us and what we can do in order to prevent this from controlling our mental health. This website is an informational outlet for those who may be struggling with their social media use. Personally, social media has affected my mental health through many of the ways that are described on this website. It is important to learn and retain the information from this website in order to better help ourselves and each other as technology and social media revolutionize. Through this website, we are able to better understand how social media can negatively impact mental health and better help ourselves.
